# Machine Learning

Welcome to the **Machine Learning** course at [Insper](https://www.insper.edu.br/).

Machine Learning is the discipline of building systems that improve with experience — programs that learn patterns from data instead of following hand-written rules. It sits at the intersection of statistics, computer science, and optimization, and it powers everything from credit scoring and medical diagnosis to search engines and large language models.

## What you will learn

This course follows a deliberate arc: **from the historical basis of the field to the edge approaches used in industry today**.

- **Foundations & History** — where ML came from: least squares, the perceptron, the AI winters, and the road to the deep learning era.
- **Working with Data** — exploratory data analysis, preprocessing, and reproducible pipelines. Most of a practitioner's time lives here.
- **Unsupervised Learning & Text** — finding structure without labels: dimensionality reduction, clustering, and modern topic modeling with BERTopic.
- **Regression & Model Evaluation** — linear models, gradient descent, regularization, and the discipline of honest validation.
- **Classification** — from k-NN and logistic regression to support vector machines, with a strong focus on metrics and imbalanced data.
- **Trees & Ensembles** — decision trees, random forests, and gradient boosting: the workhorses of tabular ML.
- **Edge Approaches** — neural networks, explainability, AutoML, MLOps, and foundation models: where the field is now and where it is going.

## Learning objectives

By the end of the course you should be able to:

1. **Frame** a real-world problem as a machine learning task and choose an appropriate family of models;
2. **Prepare** data responsibly — explore, clean, transform, and split it without leaking information;
3. **Implement** classical algorithms from first principles (linear/logistic regression, k-NN, trees) and explain the mathematics behind them;
4. **Evaluate** models rigorously with cross-validation and task-appropriate metrics;
5. **Apply** state-of-the-art tooling (scikit-learn, gradient boosting libraries, BERTopic, SHAP) to practical problems;
6. **Communicate** results and limitations honestly, including model interpretability and ethical considerations.

## How the course works

Each subject has a dedicated page with theory, worked mathematics, runnable Python examples, and visualizations. **At the end of every subject there is an interactive quiz** — use it to check your understanding before moving on.

!!! tip "Prerequisites"
    Comfort with Python, basic linear algebra (vectors, matrices), basic calculus (derivatives), and introductory probability and statistics.
